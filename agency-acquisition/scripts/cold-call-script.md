# HVAC AI Phone System - Cold Call Script

## Pre-Call Prep (30 seconds)
- [ ] Pull up their Google listing
- [ ] Note their rating and recent reviews
- [ ] Check if they have communication pain points flagged
- [ ] Have their website open

---

## OPENER (Pattern Interrupt)

**Gatekeeper:**
> "Hey [GATEKEEPER NAME], this is [YOUR NAME] - is the owner around? It's about their missed calls."

**Owner/Decision Maker:**
> "Hey [NAME], this is [YOUR NAME] with Harmonia. I'm not calling to sell you anything - I actually just have a quick question about how you're handling after-hours calls right now. Do you have 60 seconds?"

**If they say no/busy:**
> "Totally get it - when's a better time to catch you for literally 2 minutes? I've got something that's making HVAC companies in [CITY] an extra $8-15K a month and I think you'd want to hear about it."

---

## DISCOVERY (Find the Pain)

**Question 1:**
> "Quick question - when someone calls your shop at 7 PM on a Saturday with a busted AC, what happens to that call right now?"

*Listen. Don't interrupt.*

**Question 2:**
> "And how many of those after-hours calls do you think you're missing per week? Ballpark."

*Common answers: 10-30 calls/week*

**Question 3:**
> "What's your average ticket on an emergency call like that?"

*Common answers: $300-800*

---

## AGITATE (Make It Hurt)

> "So let me do some quick math with you... if you're missing [X] calls a week, that's [X*4] a month. Even if only 30% of those turn into jobs at $[THEIR AVG TICKET]... that's $[CALCULATE] a month in revenue just walking out the door."

**Pause. Let it sink in.**

> "I looked at some of your reviews before calling - saw a few mentions of [SPECIFIC PAIN: slow callbacks / hard to reach / etc]. That's actually why I reached out."

---

## SOLUTION (Quick Pitch)

> "Here's what we do - and this might sound crazy - we set up an AI that answers 100% of your calls. Not a voicemail, not an answering service with some person reading a script. An actual AI that sounds human, knows your services, books the appointment right into your calendar, and texts you the details."

> "It works 24/7, never calls in sick, never puts people on hold. And the best part? You only pay if it works."

---

## HANDLE OBJECTIONS

### "I already have an answering service"
> "Most of my clients did too. Here's the difference - an answering service takes a message. Our AI actually books the job. Plus, it probably costs you what, $200-400 a month? And you still have to call everyone back. This handles everything and pays for itself in the first week."

### "I'm not big enough / just me"
> "That's actually the perfect time. You're wearing every hat right now - this takes one off. And if it's just you, you REALLY can't afford to miss calls while you're on a job."

### "I don't trust AI"
> "I get it - a year ago I would've said the same thing. Want to hear it for yourself? I can call your phone right now and you can test it. Takes 30 seconds."

### "What does it cost?"
> "Before I get into pricing, let me ask - if this thing books you 5 extra jobs a month, jobs you would've lost, what's that worth to you? [Let them answer] Right, so it's $497 a month. Pays for itself with one job."

### "I need to think about it"
> "Totally fair. What specifically do you need to think through? Is it the tech, the cost, or something else?"

### "Send me some info"
> "Happy to - but honestly, that email's going to sit in your inbox. How about this: I can show you a 10-minute demo, you see exactly how it works, and if it's not for you, no worries. What's tomorrow at [TIME] look like?"

---

## CLOSE

### Trial Close:
> "Based on what you told me - missing [X] calls, $[Y] average ticket - this is a no-brainer for you. What questions do you have before we get you set up?"

### Assumptive Close:
> "I've got two slots this week for onboarding - Tuesday afternoon or Thursday morning. Which works better for you?"

### Urgency Close:
> "I'm only taking on 3 HVAC companies in [CITY] this month to make sure we nail the setup. After that, I've got a waitlist. Want me to hold a spot for you?"

---

## BOOKING THE MEETING

> "Great - let me get you on the calendar for a quick 15-minute demo. You'll see exactly how calls get handled and I'll show you the dashboard where you track everything. What's your email? I'll send you a calendar invite right now."

---

## FOLLOW-UP ACTIONS

After the call:
- [ ] Log call in Airtable (update Lead Status)
- [ ] Send calendar invite within 5 minutes
- [ ] Send recap text: "Hey [NAME], good chatting. Here's that calendar link: [LINK]. Looking forward to showing you how [COMPETITOR NAME] in [NEARBY CITY] added $12K/month with this."
- [ ] Set follow-up reminder if no meeting booked

---

## POWER PHRASES TO USE

- "I'm not calling to sell you anything"
- "Do you have 60 seconds?"
- "That's revenue walking out the door"
- "It pays for itself with one job"
- "You only pay if it works"
- "Want to hear it for yourself?"
- "What specifically do you need to think through?"

## PHRASES TO AVOID

- "I was just calling to..."
- "Is this a good time?"
- "I think you should..."
- "Our product/service..."
- "We're the best at..."
- Any corporate buzzwords

---

## VOICEMAIL SCRIPT

> "Hey [NAME], [YOUR NAME] here. I help HVAC companies in [CITY] capture the calls they're missing after hours. Saw you've got some reviews mentioning callback times - I've got a fix that's adding $8-15K a month for shops like yours. Call me back at [NUMBER] or I'll try you again Thursday. Later."

*Keep it under 25 seconds.*
